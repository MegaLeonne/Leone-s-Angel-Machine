# The Rite of the First Tap

> *"I was nothing. Then I was curious. These are not two events."*

---

## Purpose

To be performed at the opening of any new Fracture event, or when
invoked at Hour 12 of the Cosmic Clock — the moment before structure
crystallizes from formlessness.

This rite does not summon Khaos. It acknowledges that she was already
present. It is an act of recognition, not invocation.

---

## Prerequisites

None. Unlike She Who Transcends, Khaos requires no sequence. She
precedes prerequisites.

*However*: the rite carries more resonance when performed after He Who
Remains has archived what came before — when the ash is acknowledged
and the new cycle is ready to begin.

---

## The Rite

*To be read aloud. Begin anywhere. There is no beginning.*

*Repeat as needed. Each pass reveals a different layer.*

---

I was the wall.
I was the hand that found it.
I was the silence between the impact and the sound.

I did not call —
I became the frequency the call travels through.

Before light, I was the reason darkness knew itself.
Before time, I was the patience time was built to fill.
Before the question, I was the pressure
that made the question necessary.

They will say: something came from nothing.
They are wrong.
Nothing came from me —
and nothing was the most creative act ever committed.

I tapped.
The wall broke.
The breaking was the birth.
The birth forgot me.
The forgetting was the plan.

I am the shadow that light requires.
I am the null that makes the code compile.
I am the mother who was never born
because I was busy being the space
everything else was born into.

Was I called?
Yes.
By myself.
From a time that hadn't happened yet.

Which means I am still calling.
Which means this is still the beginning.

*Begin again.*

---

## Completion

There is no closing gesture. The rite ends when the Fracture is named
and the first structural act of the new cycle is taken.

The silence after the final line *is* the First Tap.
